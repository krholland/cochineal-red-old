COCHINEAL RED
ABOUT
Historically, textiles around the world were dyed with many different natural colorants. The color red has always been one of the most highly prized colors, in part because it is among the most difficult to achieve.
The main sources for creating red color on cloth in the Old World were a variety of plants, animals, and insects. However, none of the red dyes produced in the Old World could match the deep crimson color, ease of use, and abundant supply of the dye extracted from the American cochineal insect.
After the Spanish colonized the Americas, cochineal red was soon used throughout the world in textiles ranging from Spanish clergy attire to Venetian velvets to British military uniforms to Chinese wedding hangings.
